## Photo Blog?

{% include image_caption.html imageurl="/images/posts/2026-07-03.jpg" title="" caption="This is caption" %}

It's very 'trendy' to go all analogue at the moment, a wholesale rejection of the modern digital world. Many people are concerned with doom scrolling and phone addictions, but that isn't what has pushed me into the following path. To save a lot of poorly crafted waffling I feel that the current 'low friction' environment that we inhabit has taken some of the joy of a great number of activities that I used to really enjoy. Everything is so easy it has become boring. 

One of these activities that really suffered for me is/was photography.

I used to LOVE taking photographs, I recall getting my first digital camera as a teenager for Christmas, it was a cannon powershot A60 a 2MP AA battery powered lump. I loved taking photos with that thing, the results were not  perfect, but it required some effort to get a half decent photo out of it, there was a bit of what I think is called 'friction' and that made getting something good a worthwhile exercise.

It was also quite manual,  if you wanted to have control over the aperture etc you had to use the dials and buttons on the back, not quite as manual as a film SLR, but still 'fun'.

My current mid range android phone wipes the floor with pretty much of my old dedicated cameras, you just point and press and get a perfectly exposed, 50mp, super sharp and nice colour image.

And because it is so easy, it’s the norm, and because it’s the norm, its boring and because its boring, I don't take 'photos' any more. I just do a few snapshots of family days out and holiday and part numbers and receipts and nothing arty.

I felt sad at that realisation and started to think what I could do to 'get into' photography again.

I found a couple of cameras in an E-waste bin at work, neither were complete, but I thought I could get them going again with not too much fuss.

A Lumix DMC-FS40 (14MP - super slim, doesn’t charge via USB!)

And

An Olympus FE-210 (7.1MP - AA batteries, XD card :blug: - chunky)

I'm going to play with these over the next coming days/weeks/months and just enjoying taking photos again. I'll post images here, with the rule of no retouching the outputs, just a bit of cropping if needed.i
